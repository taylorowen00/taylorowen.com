If you spend any time listening to the people running AI companies, you'll notice that they're pretty fond of making wild predictions about the future.

They've told us that AI will cure cancer, eliminate drudgery, and solve climate change. And yet — by and large — these kinds of big ideas haven't really materialized.

Sure, we can code a website or write an article in just a couple of minutes…but where are the crazy, radical moonshots we've been promised? The ones that will transform the world?

Well, I think Aza Raskin might have one of those ideas.

Raskin is the president of the Center for Humane Technology and co-founder of something called the Earth Species Project, where they're using machine learning to understand…animals.

It's almost hard to articulate how transformational that would be. Raskin and his colleagues are imagining a world where birds get to vote in elections and dolphins represent themselves in court.

That might sound hard to believe — but Raskin says he's not that far off from making it a reality.

So I wanted to talk to him about this crazy moonshot idea — and what happens to our world when animals…have the right to speak.
